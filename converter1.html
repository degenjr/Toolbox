Tips
  • Make sure you have the proper octane level of gas. Having an octane level below or above your engine's specifications may cause it to flood.

  • Don't mix the gas and oil together within the confines of the gas tank of your piece of equipment. This could create future problems in your equipment's carburetor, fuel lines, and the like.

  • Make sure your gas is fresh. If it happens to be over 30 days old, use a stabilizer to help prevent engine malfunctions.
