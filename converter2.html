Tips
  • Apply your fertilizer after rainy periods so that it can be easily absorbed by the grass and also not burn it up.

  • Depending on your location, use the correct amount of fertilizer for different seasons. For example, southern climates require more fertilizer in the early to late spring time period compared to that of nothern climates which require more fertilizer in the fall growing season.

  • Be careful not to use too much nitrogen. Excess nitrogen can burn up your lawn if it is not absorbed quickly and it could lead to water table contamination.

  • Make sure to follow all manufacture instructions when it comes to applying the fertilizer to your lawn.

  • Do not allow people (especially children) or pets to walk through a freshly fertilized lawn. The chemicals in fertilizers are extremely toxic to pets and can cause severe illness in children if injested.
