Tips
  • Make sure to drill the hole with the proper drill bit with the proper amount of speed. For example, hardwood requires a slower drilling speed so that it does not scortch the wood or wear out the bit.

  • Watch how deep you are drilling. If need be, apply a collar or some other means of gauging device to help you better determine when to stop drilling.

  • Use a smaller bit to drill a pilot hole that way it decreases the chance of the bigger bit from potentially slipping on the material.
